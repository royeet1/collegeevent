About the symposium

Where the department's brightest ideas leave the lab.

VERTEX'26 is the flagship technical symposium organized by the Department of Computer Science & Engineering. Every year, it brings students from across the region together for two days of competitive coding, original research, and rapid-fire product building.

The theme for this edition, "Converge. Create. Compute.", reflects a simple idea: the best technical work happens when disciplines — algorithms, research, and design — meet in the same room and are put under a deadline.

Organized entirely by the Student Technical Council under faculty guidance, VERTEX is run by students, for students — from problem-setting to judging to the closing ceremony.

Previous editions

  • 2025 VERTEX'25 drew 950+ participants from 40 colleges, with the coding track judged live on a national leaderboard.
  • 2024 First year to introduce the Web Development sprint — 60 teams shipped a working product in 24 hours.
  • 2023 Paper Presentation track expanded to four domains, publishing top entries in the department's technical bulletin.
  • 2022 The inaugural edition — a single-day event with 300 participants and three events.
Organized by

Department of Computer Science & Engineering

The department runs undergraduate and postgraduate programs in computer science, with active research groups in AI/ML, distributed systems, and cybersecurity — the same groups that set this year's paper-presentation problem statements.

Faculty Mentorship

Every track is anchored by a faculty coordinator who reviews problem statements and judging rubrics before the event.

Student-Run Council

A 40-member Student Technical Council handles logistics, judging panels, sponsorships, and on-ground execution.

Industry Ties

Keynotes and mentorship sessions are led by engineers and researchers from partner companies and alumni.

Good to know

Frequently asked

Who can participate?

Undergraduate and postgraduate students from any recognized college or university. Team sizes vary by event — check the schedule page for details.

Is there a registration fee?

Most events are free for students from the host college; a nominal fee applies for outstation participants, covering kits and refreshments.

Do I need to pick one track only?

No — tracks run in parallel, so you can register for multiple events as long as timings don't overlap.
